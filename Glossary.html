HUMAN ANATOMY GLOSSARY

Anatomy studio

•Anatomy is the study of the structure and organization of the body and its parts.

•The study of how the organs and systems of the body function and work together to maintain life.

•A group of cells that perform a specific function or functions together.

•An individual living being that exhibits all the characteristics of life, including growth, reproduction, response to stimuli, and metabolism.

•A structure composed of different tissues that work together to carry out specific functions in the body.

•A group of organs and tissues that work together to perform a specific physiological function.

•Body's stable internal conditions.

•Basic unit of life.

•The body system composed of bones, cartilage, ligaments, and other connective tissues that provides support, protection, and structure to the body and facilitates movement.

•The system composed of muscles and tendons that enables movement of the body and its parts, maintains posture, and generates heat.

•The system composed of the brain, spinal cord, nerves, and sensory organs that coordinates and controls bodily functions and responds to internal and external stimuli.

•The system composed of the heart, blood vessels, and blood that transports oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and waste products throughout the body.

•The system composed of the lungs and airways that exchanges oxygen and carbon dioxide between the body and the environment.

•The system composed of the mouth, esophagus, stomach, intestines, and other organs that breaks down food into nutrients, absorbs nutrients into the bloodstream, and eliminates waste products.

•The system composed of glands that secrete hormones into the bloodstream to regulate various physiological processes, including metabolism, growth, and reproduction.

•The system composed of organs and structures involved in reproduction, including the gonads (testes in males, ovaries in females), external genitalia, and associated structures.

•The system composed of the skin, hair, nails, and glands that protects the body from external threats, regulates temperature, and synthesizes vitamin D.

•The system composed of cells, tissues, and organs that defends the body against pathogens, foreign substances, and abnormal cells.

•The system composed of the kidneys, ureters, bladder, and urethra that removes waste products from the blood, regulates electrolyte balance, and maintains fluid balance.

•The systems involved in sensation, including the visual (eyes), auditory (ears), olfactory (nose), gustatory (tongue), and somatosensory (touch) systems.
